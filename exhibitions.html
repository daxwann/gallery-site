Goya's Prints
12.10.17 - 03.01.18

Portraits by Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec
12.15.17 - 03.10.18

Xiaoda Wang Gallery presents a collection of prints produced by the Spanish artist Francisco José de Goya y Lucientes (1746-1828). This exhibition includes etchings from the two iconic series "Los Caprichos (The Caprices)" and "Los Desastres de la Guerra (The Disasters of War)."

Published in 1799, the prints from "Los Caprichos" depict various vices and injustice seen during this turbulent time in Spain. Goya would use humor and metaphors to criticize the widespread moral failings of the government, church, and society.

Goya produced "the Disasters of War" series to document the horrors of the Napoleonic invasion in 1808. In contrast to the fantastic allegories of "Los Caprichos," these Disasters of War" etchings are realistic depictions of the casualties of war, consequently blurring the line between art and journalism.

Curated by Xiaoda Wang.

This exhibition on the portraits by Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec takes the visitor into the social millieu of fin-de-siecle Paris. Born in 1864, Toulouse-Lautrec was a French painter and printmaker famous for the vibrant posters that became the symbol of Parisian theatre in the late-nineteeth century.

Toulouse-Lautrec captured the artists, actors, singers, dancers, and patrons in their natural habitats, which were bars, cafes, nightclubs, and theatres. Toulouse-Lautrec used lithography as a way to mass produce his colorful prints in large sizes.

Curated by Xiaoda Wang
